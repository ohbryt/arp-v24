## Core vs Option Paths

**Core Path:** Single-agent PF14–siYARS2 proof-of-mechanism in NSCLC

**Option Paths (behind gates):**
- Inhaled delivery engineering
- Combination ferroptosis amplification (siKDM4A, siDGAT1, sulfasalazine)

**Critical Insight:** PF14 lung signal ≠ tumour delivery. First budget tranche must buy down:
1. **NSCLC dependency** — YARS2 biology transferability from CRC
2. **Tumour vs normal-lung target engagement** — explicit measurement required
3. **Repeatable efficacy/tolerability** — confirmatory studies

**Scientific:** Dependency replication → Formulation qualification → Mechanism confirmation → Target engagement → Efficacy/tolerability

**Translational:** Analytical control → Route decision → Nonclinical safety scoping → Partner/funding package

## Key Risk Register

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| YARS2 not reproducible in NSCLC | Medium | High | Require ≥2 NSCLC contexts + normal comparators |
| Lung signal ≠ tumour delivery | High | High | Pilot study with tumour/normal-lung/liver panel |
| Biomarker misclassification | Medium | High | Orthogonal RNA + protein + functional assays |
| PF14-siRNA instability | Medium | High | N/P 2-4 qualification matrix |
| Combination overwhelms core | Medium | Medium | Core-path rule; option path only after Gate Beta |
| Normal lung/liver toxicity | Medium | High | Sentinel tolerability cohort + stopping rules |
