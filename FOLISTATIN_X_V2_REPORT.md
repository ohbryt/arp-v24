## FoliStatin-X v2 Formulation

### Component Details

#### 1. (-)-Epicatechin (Primary)
- **Source:** Camellia sinensis (Green Tea)
- **Dose:** 1mg/kg/day (divided 2x/day)
- **Mechanism:** MSTN↓, Follistatin↑, FOXO1 modulation
- **Clinical Evidence:** 62 patients, 8 weeks, significant muscle strength improvement
- **Key Finding:** ↑ muscle strength, ↓ myostatin, ↑ follistatin

#### 2. Epicatechin Gallate (ECG)
- **Source:** Camellia sinensis (Green Tea)
- **Dose:** 200-400mg/day (divided 2x/day)
- **Mechanism:** Pro-differentiation effect (strongest among catechins)
- **2025 Study:** Strongest pro-differentiation effect among all catechin monomers

#### 3. Ursolic Acid
- **Source:** Apple peel, Rosemary, Basil
- **Dose:** 250-500mg/day (divided 2x/day with fatty meals)
- **Mechanism:** AMPK/PGC-1α activation, mitochondrial biogenesis, UCP3-mediated fatty acid oxidation
- **Preclinical:** ↑ mitochondrial mass and ATP generation in C2C12 myotubes

#### 4. Fertilized Chicken Yolk Extract
- **Source:** Fertilized chicken eggs
- **Dose:** TBD (once daily, morning)
- **Mechanism:** Direct myostatin inhibition, novel bioactive peptides
- **2026 Novel:** Emerging evidence for natural myostatin inhibition

#### 5. Resveratrol (Trans)
- **Source:** Vitis vinifera (Grapes), Polygonum cuspidatum
- **Dose:** 150-500mg/day (divided 2x/day)
- **Mechanism:** SIRT1 activation, AMPK activation, mitochondrial biogenesis
- **Evidence:** ↑ mitochondrial function, ↑ insulin sensitivity

#### 6. Berberine
- **Source:** Berberis vulgaris (Barberry)
- **Dose:** 500-1,500mg/day (divided 3x/day with meals)
- **Mechanism:** AMPK activation, insulin sensitivity, mitochondrial function
- **Note:** Low bioavailability (5%) - recommend liposomal formulation

## Clinical Evidence Summary

### Epicatechin RCT (Level 1 Evidence)
- **Trial:** NCT02528357
- **Patients:** 62 males with sarcopenia (68.63 ± 2.86 years)
- **Design:** 8-week randomized controlled trial
- **Intervention:** Epicatechin 1mg/kg/day
- **Results:**
  - ↑ skeletal muscle strength
  - ↓ plasma myostatin levels
  - ↑ plasma follistatin levels
- **Conclusion:** "Epicatechin may thus hold promise as treatment for sarcopenia"

### Tea Catechin RCT
- **Participants:** 128 women with sarcopenia
- **Design:** 4 groups (exercise + tea catechin, exercise only, tea catechin only, control)
- **Duration:** 3 months
- **Dose:** 350mL/day green tea
- **Results:** ↑ muscle mass with exercise intervention

### Ursolic Acid Preclinical
- **Study:** Chu et al. 2015, Mol Nutr Food Res
- **Model:** C2C12 myotubes
- **Results:**
  - ↑ mitochondrial mass
  - ↑ ATP generation capacity
  - ↑ UCP3 expression via AMPK/PGC-1α pathway

### ECG 2025 Study
- **Model:** C57BL/6J mice
- **Finding:** ECG had stronger pro-differentiation effect than other catechin monomers
- **Dose-response:** Effect increased with concentration

## Formulation Notes & Optimization

### Bioavailability Enhancement
1. **Berberine:** Use liposomal formulation (5% → 50%+ bioavailability)
2. **Curcumin:** Combine with piperine 5mg (15% → 50%+ bioavailability)
3. **Resveratrol:** Nanocrystal or self-emulsifying formulation

### Dosing Protocol
- **Start:** Lower doses, titrate up over 2 weeks
- **Timing:** With meals (especially fatty meals for ursolic acid)
- **Duration:** Minimum 8-12 weeks for initial effects
- **Frequency:** Divided doses (2-3x/day) for optimal absorption

### Monitoring
- **Baseline:** Muscle mass (DXA), strength testing, inflammatory markers
- **Week 4:** Check tolerance and initial response
- **Week 8-12:** Full outcome assessment
- **Ongoing:** Liver function with high-dose epicatechin

## Safety Profile

### Overall Assessment: Excellent

All components have established safety profiles:
- **Epicatechin:** FDA GRAS, used in supplements
- **Ursolic Acid:** Common in food, well-tolerated
- **Resveratrol:** Extensively studied, safe up to 2g/day
- **Berberine:** Traditional medicine use, safe at recommended doses
- **Curcumin:** FDA GRAS, common food ingredient

### Contraindications & Precautions
1. **Pregnancy/Nursing:** Not recommended (insufficient data)
2. **Liver Disease:** Monitor with high-dose epicatechin
3. **Surgery:** Discontinue 2 weeks before surgery
4. **Anticoagulants:** May enhance bleeding risk (berberine)
