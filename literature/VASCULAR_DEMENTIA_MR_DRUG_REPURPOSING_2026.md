## Executive Summary

This study applies **drug target Mendelian randomization (MR)** to identify existing drugs that could be repurposed for **vascular dementia (VaD)** prevention.

**Key Findings:**
- Causal risk factors identified: blood pressure, LDL-cholesterol, systemic inflammation (CRP)
- Drug repurposing strategy leverages established safety profiles
- Genetic evidence increases probability of regulatory approval (>2x)

## Background

### Vascular Dementia (VaD) Burden

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Global dementia cases** | >55 million |
| **VaD cases with cerebrovascular evidence** | ~80% of all dementia |
| **Disease mechanism** | Progressive cerebrovascular disease → brain injury from impaired cerebral blood flow |
| **Current treatments** | None effective; only risk factor modification (cholesterol, BP) |

### Unmet Need

> *"Vascular dementia has received comparatively little attention and research funding."*

- No effective disease-modifying treatments
- Only symptomatic management available
- Prevention trials in midlife are long and costly

## Methodology: Drug Target Mendelian Randomization

### What is Drug Target MR?

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Instrumental Variables (IVs)** | Genetic variants within or near genes encoding drug targets |
| **Principle** | Genotypes are randomly allocated at conception (like RCT randomization) |
| **Advantage** | Avoids confounding and reverse causation biases of observational studies |
| **Applications** | Target validation, drug repurposing, clinical trial design |

### Analogy to Randomized Trials

```
Observational Study:                    Mendelian Randomization:
Confounding ←─┐                         Genetic allocation (IV) 
       ↓      │                                  ↓
Exposure → Outcome                   Genetic variant → Protein function → Disease
              ↑                                      ↑
       Reverse causation                    No reverse causation
               ↓                                      ↓
         BIASED                                   UNBIASED
```

### Previous Success: IL-6R Inhibitors for COVID-19

| Example | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| **IL-6R inhibitors** | MR-supported repurposing → approved for severe COVID-19 |
| **Success rate** | Therapeutics with genetic support are **>2x more likely** to achieve regulatory approval |

### Rationale for Each Category

#### Blood Pressure
- Prior GWAS suggests BP is a **causal risk factor** for VaD
- Antihypertensive drugs could reduce VaD incidence

#### LDL-Cholesterol
- Lipid-lowering therapies may protect cerebral vasculature
- LDL-c lowering → reduced cerebrovascular pathology

#### Systemic Inflammation
- CRP levels associated with VaD neuroimaging markers
- Anti-inflammatory drugs (e.g., IL-6R antagonists) as potential intervention

## Study Design Considerations

### Traditional Trial Limitations

| Issue | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| **Small sample sizes** | Most trials n < 500 |
| **Elderly-only cohorts** | Restricted to >70 years old |
| **Long follow-up needed** | Prevention trials in midlife not cost-effective |
| **Prodromal pathology starts midlife** | Early intervention likely more effective |

### MR Advantages for VaD

| Advantage | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Cost-effective** | Uses existing genetic databases |
| **Short timeline** | No need for decades-long follow-up |
| **Midlife assessment** | Genetic effects span lifetime |
| **Causal inference** | IV approach avoids observational biases |

## Drug Repurposing Framework

### Steps in Drug Target MR

```
1. Identify drug targets with genetic support
        ↓
2. Find genetic variants (SNPs) near target genes
        ↓
3. Extract GWAS summary statistics for disease/outcome
        ↓
4. Perform MR analysis (inverse-variance weighted, etc.)
        ↓
5. Evaluate:
   - Effect size
   - Direction of effect (protective vs harmful)
   - Pleiotropy assessment
        ↓
6. Prioritize candidates for clinical development
```

### Repurposing Criteria

| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Genetic association** | p < 5×10⁻⁸ (genome-wide significance) |
| **Effect direction** | Consistent with therapeutic mechanism |
| **No pleiotropy** | Horizontal pleiotropy < acceptable level |
| **Effect magnitude** | Clinically meaningful (HR < 0.8 or similar) |
| **Safety profile** | Existing drug has acceptable safety |
