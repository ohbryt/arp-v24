### 3. Molecular Mechanisms of Progression (800-1000 words)
#### 3.1 Iron Overload and Ferroptosis
**Key concept:** Metabolic hyperferritinemia (MHF) is central to MASLD progression

- Iron accumulation in reticuloendothelial system (RES)
- Dysregulated iron distribution → hepatic iron overload
- High-fat diet + iron → oxidative stress → inflammation → HSC activation → fibrosis

**Ferroptosis mechanism:**
- Iron-dependent lipid peroxidation (cite: Anastasopoulos et al. 2025, Cancers)
- GPX4 inactivation → accumulation of 4-HNE and lipid ROS
- depletion of hepatic antioxidant capacity
- Role in MASH and MASH-related HCC (tumor microenvironment alterations, ferroptosis resistance)

#### 3.2 Key Regulators

| Regulator | Function | Ref |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **GPX4** | Lipid peroxidase repair | Tang et al. 2024, Hepatology |
| **SLC7A11** | Cystine transport, glutathione synthesis | Feng et al. 2022, Liver Int |
| **FSP1** | Ferroptosis inhibitor (NADPH:quinone oxidoreductase) | ARP v24 research |
| **ACSL4** | Lipid peroxidation substrate | Cheng et al. 2023, Front Nutr |
| **NCOA4** | Ferritinophagy, iron release | Liu et al. 2022, Front Pharmacol |
| **Hepcidin** | Iron regulation (↓ in MASH) | Chen et al. 2022, J Clin Transl Hepatol |

#### 3.3 Metabolic Reprogramming
- Lipid accumulation → lipotoxicity
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- ER stress
- Glucose metabolism dysregulation (Warburg effect)

#### 3.4 Inflammation and Fibrosis
- NF-κB pathway activation
- Cytokine cascade (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1β)
- Hepatic stellate cell (HSC) activation
- Transforming growth factor-β (TGF-β)

#### 3.5 Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition (EMT)
- Iron-induced EMT in hepatocarcinogenesis
- DNA damage repair impairment
- Wnt/β-catenin pathway dysregulation
