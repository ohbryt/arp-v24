01 · Executive Summary

Executive Summary

Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis (IPF) affects approximately 5 million patients worldwide with a median survival of only 3-5 years. While two FDA-approved antifibrotic drugs (pirfenidone and nintedanib) exist, neither reverses disease progression—highlighting a critical unmet medical need.

  • The clinical pipeline spans multiple mechanisms: Hedgehog inhibition (Taladegib), integrin targeting (BEACON-IPF), and cell therapy (REGEND001)
  • Emerging targets include SIRT3/6/7, COL1A1, SP1, and PI3K-AKT pathway components

02 · Introduction

Introduction & Epidemiology

Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis (IPF) is a chronic, progressive, and ultimately fatal interstitial lung disease characterized by persistent alveolar inflammation and extensive fibrotic changes that progressively impair lung function.

Disease Overview

IPF results from a combination of genetic predisposition, environmental factors, and aberrant cellular responses to injury. The disease follows a progressive course, ultimately leading to respiratory failure and death. Median survival is 3-5 years after diagnosis, with most patients dying of respiratory causes.

Epidemiology

Global Burden
~5 million patients worldwide
$10+ billion annual US healthcare costs
Patient Demographics
Diagnosis: 6th decade of life
Male predominance
Higher incidence in smokers

Unmet Need

Current therapies slow disease progression but do not reverse fibrosis. Lung transplantation remains the only curative option, but is limited by donor availability and surgical risk.
03 · Molecular Mechanisms

Molecular Mechanisms

IPF involves dysregulation of multiple interconnected signaling pathways that drive fibroblast activation, extracellular matrix deposition, and progressive fibrosis.

Key Pathways

PI3K-AKT Pathway

Plays a crucial role in cell survival, proliferation, and differentiation. Aberrant activation leads to increased expression of downstream targets (mTOR, S6K). Astragaloside IV exerts protective effects through PIK3CA targeting.

Hedgehog (Hh) Signaling

Involved in embryonic development and tissue repair. In IPF, Hh pathway is aberrantly activated, leading to proliferation and differentiation of fibroblasts and deposition of extracellular matrix. Target of Taladegib.

TGF-β Pathway

Central fibrogenic cytokine in IPF. Stimulates differentiation of fibroblasts into myofibroblasts—the key effector cells in the fibrotic process. Primary target of pirfenidone and nintedanib.

Wnt/β-Catenin Pathway

Aberrant activation implicated in IPF with increased expression of cyclin D1 and c-myc. Contributes to fibroblast proliferation and persistence.

Emerging Mechanisms

SIRTuins (SIRT3/6/7)

NAD+-dependent deacetylases regulating cellular metabolism, stress resistance, and longevity. Targeting the PGC1-alpha/Sirt3 pathway, SOD2, and OGG1 represents potential therapeutic strategies.

Cellular Senescence

Accumulation of senescent cells promotes fibrosis through the Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (SASP)—releasing pro-fibrotic factors including cytokines, growth factors, and proteases.

COL1A1 & SP1

COL1A1 (type I collagen) is a hub protein regulated by lncRNA H19. SP1 transcription factor inhibition at DNA level reduces lung fibrosis.

04 · Current Approved Therapies

Current Approved Therapies

Two antifibrotic therapies are currently FDA-approved for IPF, both demonstrating modest efficacy in slowing disease progression.

Pirfenidone

Mechanism & Efficacy
Mechanism: Inhibits TGF-β signaling; anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic properties
Efficacy: ~700 mL FVC decline over 5 years
Side Effects: GI symptoms, photosensitivity, liver toxicity

Nintedanib

Mechanism & Efficacy
Mechanism: Tyrosine kinase inhibitor targeting TGF-β, PDGF, and VEGF pathways
Efficacy: ~660 mL FVC decline over 5 years (40 mL difference vs pirfenidone, not statistically significant)
Side Effects: Diarrhea, liver enzyme elevations, bleeding risk

05 · Clinical Pipeline 2024-2025

Clinical Pipeline 2024-2025

Multiple novel agents are advancing through clinical development, targeting diverse mechanisms beyond the current TGF-β-centric approaches.

Phase 2a: AI-Discovered TNIK Inhibitor

Rentosertib (ISM001-055) — Nature Medicine, June 2025
Significance: First AI-generated small-molecule inhibitor to reach human Phase 2a trials in IPF
Target: TNIK (Traf2 and NCK-interacting kinase)
Design: Multicenter, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled
Results: Improved lung function and reduced fibrosis
Discovery: Generated using generative AI — a first-in-class approach

Other Pipeline Programs

Taladegib (Endeavor BioMedicines)

Hedgehog signaling pathway inhibitor that disrupts Hh pathway to prevent accumulation of myofibroblasts responsible for fibrosis.

BEACON-IPF (Pliant Therapeutics)

αvβ6 integrin inhibitor targeting integrin-mediated TGF-β activation. Phase 2b/3 trial in IPF patients.

FIBRONEER-IPF (Phase 3)

Phase 3 trial (NCT05321069) in patients with or without prior antifibrotic therapy. Novel antifibrotic mechanism targeting LOXL2 (collagen cross-linking enzyme).

REGEND001 (Cell Therapy)

Autologous transplantation of P63+ lung progenitor cells for lung tissue repair. Phase 1 clinical trial (European Respiratory Society).

Combination Therapy: INJOURNEY Trial

Tests nintedanib with add-on pirfenidone to evaluate safety of combination. Combined adverse effects remain a concern limiting this approach.

06 · Emerging Targets & Natural Products

Emerging Targets & Natural Products

Natural Product: Astragaloside IV

Astragaloside IV (AS-IV) — Scientific Reports 2025
Source: Astragalus membranaceus (Traditional Chinese Medicine)
Mechanism: Modulates PI3K-AKT pathway by targeting PIK3CA
Evidence: Reduced collagen accumulation in bleomycin-induced mouse model; significant pulmonary fibrosis reduction
Potential: Novel antifibrotic therapeutic agent

Novel Small Molecules

SIRT3/6/7 Activators

Targeting SIRTuins and the PGC1-alpha/Sirt3 pathway, SOD2, and OGG1 may provide potential strategies for pulmonary fibrosis treatment (Frontiers, March 2025).

COL1A1 Targeting

Type I collagen (COL1A1) is a hub protein regulated by lncRNA H19. Inhibition of SP1 activity at the DNA level is an effective approach for treatment of lung fibrosis.

Future Directions

07 · Conclusion

Conclusion

IPF remains a devastating disease with limited treatment options. The 2024-2025 pipeline offers renewed hope through diverse mechanisms and novel approaches.

Summary of Key Advances

  1. AI-discovered drugs (Rentosertib) have entered Phase 2a clinical trials—marking a milestone in generative chemistry
  2. Multiple mechanism-targeted approaches are in development: Hh inhibition, integrin targeting, cell therapy
  3. Combination therapy strategies are being explored despite safety concerns
  4. Natural products like Astragaloside IV provide novel antifibrotic mechanisms
  5. Regenerative medicine approaches (P63+ progenitor cells) offer potential disease modification

Unmet Needs & Opportunities

Critical Unmet Need: No approved therapy reverses IPF progression. Current antifibrotics slow decline but cannot restore lung function.
